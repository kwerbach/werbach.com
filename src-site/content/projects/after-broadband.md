## Workshop Overview

In April 2012, the Wharton School and the Institute for the Future convened a one-day workshop in San Francisco that brought together a group of leading technologists, entrepreneurs, academics, and policymakers to explore the future of broadband over the next decade.

## Vision for Hyperconnectivity

The workshop examined how dramatically increased bandwidth and ubiquitous connectivity would transform:

### Technology Infrastructure
- Evolution beyond traditional broadband architectures
- Mobile and wireless connectivity advances
- The Internet of Things and sensor networks
- Cloud computing and distributed systems

### Economic Implications
- New business models enabled by high-bandwidth applications
- Transformation of industries from healthcare to education
- The future of work and remote collaboration
- Global economic competitiveness

### Social and Cultural Impact
- Changes in how people communicate and form communities
- Access and equity in a hyperconnected world
- Privacy and security in always-connected environments
- Digital divide and inclusion challenges

### Policy Considerations
- Regulatory frameworks for next-generation networks
- Spectrum allocation and management
- Net neutrality and open internet principles
- International coordination and governance

## Participants

The workshop convened:
- Technology visionaries and industry leaders
- Academic researchers in networking and telecommunications
- Policymakers and regulators
- Civil society representatives
- Entrepreneurs building next-generation applications

## Forward-Looking Insights

The workshop generated valuable foresight about:
- The shift toward mobile-first connectivity
- The emergence of streaming video as the dominant application
- Growing importance of data centers and edge computing
- Challenges of managing increasingly complex network systems

## Legacy

Many of the trends and challenges identified in the workshop have become central to current debates about digital infrastructure, connectivity equity, and the future of the internet.
