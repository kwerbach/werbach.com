## Summit Overview

In April 2015, the Wharton School hosted a major gamification summit titled "Gameful Approaches to Motivation and Engagement" (#WhartonGAME). The event brought together many of the most influential thought-leaders in both the academic and practitioner space in the arena of gamification.

## Focus Areas

The summit explored cutting-edge research and practical applications of gamification across multiple domains:

### Theoretical Foundations
- The psychology of motivation and engagement
- Self-Determination Theory and intrinsic motivation
- Behavioral economics and game design

### Business Applications
- Employee engagement and productivity
- Customer loyalty and retention
- Innovation and problem-solving

### Educational Context
- Learning and skill development
- Student engagement and achievement
- Adaptive learning systems

### Social Impact
- Health and wellness programs
- Civic engagement and social change
- Environmental sustainability

## Key Participants

The summit featured:
- Leading academic researchers in game design, psychology, and education
- Practitioners from Fortune 500 companies implementing gamification
- Game designers and user experience experts
- Entrepreneurs building gamification platforms

## Outcomes

The #WhartonGAME summit advanced the conversation about responsible and effective gamification, emphasizing the importance of:
- Understanding intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation
- Avoiding exploitative or manipulative design
- Measuring meaningful outcomes beyond points and badges
- Building sustainable engagement systems

## Impact

The summit contributed to the maturation of gamification as both an academic field and professional practice, helping establish best practices and ethical guidelines for gameful design.
