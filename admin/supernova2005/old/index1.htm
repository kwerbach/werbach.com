Welcome to Supernova 2005!
June 20-22, San Francisco, CA

The Supernova conference has always been about making connections in an increasingly distributed world. Industry leaders agree, it is the place where...

• Technology's top thought leaders and innovators debate the emerging trends in decentralized computing, media and communications

• Over 250 prominent technologists, venture capitalists, business leaders and media come to network

• Supernova community members across the world interact online using cutting-edge social software


What's new in 2005: A lot!

Business leadership
We're pleased to announce that Supernova is now produced in partnership with Wharton Business School, where host, Kevin Werbach, recently joined the faculty. This year's Conference will bring together both technology and business leaders to provide varied viewpoints on decentralization trends.

New programs
The agenda now includes the Wharton West Workshops and Technology Showcase - a day of emerging technology workshops targeted to technologists and business professionals, along with an opening Conference networking event, all held at Wharton's West Coast state-of-the-art facility, on June 20th, in San Francisco, CA. Go to Wharton West's Web site to tour the facility.

An exclusive theme
Connecting Worlds through Connected Platforms. In a decentralized world, the network itself is the single platform for commerce, social interaction, work and entertainment. At Supernova, we'll focus on exploring the next generation of "connected" technologies not only in a social, but a business context, and how they'll impact employees, industries, and customers.

Fresh perspectives
We'll examine how corporations and individuals are leveraging social software, mobile devices, and online gaming environments, and ways that communities can maximize the latent value of their network connections. Topics include:

distributed e-commerce • mobile applications • the new power of the "long tail" in commerce and media • massively multiplayer virtual worlds • business blogging • pervasive wireless sensors • rich media collaboration • voice over IP applications • and more
