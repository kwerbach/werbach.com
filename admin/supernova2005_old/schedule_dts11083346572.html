Disruptive Wireless
Friday, June 25, 2004, 11:45am - 12:45

Wireless connectivity will be the backbone of the emerging pervasive Internet. New technologies, unleashed by new regulatory approaches, promise an explosion of wireless communications capacity unlike anything seen before. Disruptive innovations range from low-power ad hoc sensor nets to ultra-wideband video streaming links to metropolitan-area data networks. How real are these scenarios, and what roadblocks stand in their way? Are unlicensed technologies such as WiFi and WiMax a threat to the 3G vision of wireless operators, or the mobile industry’s true future? Will software radio and other edge innovations shift the balance of power in the wireless world?


Vanu Bose, CEO, Vanu, Inc.

Vanu is the President and CEO of Vanu, Inc., a company that he co-founded in 1998 to provide waveform software and design consulting services for software radio systems. He has been an invited speaker and panelist at both government and industry events. In addition to numerous publications and talks, he has several pending patents for his work in software radio. Vanu earned all of his degrees from MIT, receiving his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science in 1999, his Master's degree in EECS in 1992, and two Bachelor's degrees, one in EECS and one in Math, in 1988.


Michael Gallagher, Acting Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Communications and, US Department of Commerce

Michael D. Gallagher was nominated by President George W. Bush on October 14, 2003 to be Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Communications and Information and Administrator of the National Telecommunications and Information Administration. As the President's principal advisor on telecommunications policy, Mr. Gallagher is tasked
with leading President Bush's initiative to develop a radio spectrum policy for the 21st Century that will better manage the nation's airwaves, enhance homeland and economic security, increase benefits to Americans, and ensure U.S. leadership in high-tech innovation. Mr.
Gallagher received his B.A. from the University of California at
Berkeley and his J.D. from the University of California in Los Angeles. He is a member of the Washington State Bar.


Tren Griffin, Telecom Evangelist, Microsoft

"Tren Griffin is Telecom Strategist in the Office of Microsoft Corporation's CTO. Mr. Griffin focuses on planning and analysis of telecommunications and network services for Microsoft Corporation in both wired and wireless environments. Mr. Griffin's role includes overseeing strategic initiatives relating to the cellular, Wi-Fi, Wi-Max, USB, and wire line industries, and formulating communications policy strategy. He also works closely with the CTO in identifying and cultivating leading telecommunications innovators. He has been involved in the wireless communications industry since joining one of the world's first cellular companies in 1998 and until joining Microsoft worked at a range of companies controlled by wireless telecom pioneer Craig McCaw."


Rob Poor, CTO, Ember

Prior to founding Ember, Robert Poor was a doctoral student at the MIT Media
Lab, where he developed Embedded Networking, a new class of wireless network that enables inexpensive, scalable, easily deployed connections for common manufactured goods. Poor has accrued over twenty-five years of technical and management-level experience from Silicon Valley including LucasFilm's precursor to Pixar, Stanford Artificial Intelligence, Lucid, NeXT, and Opcode. He has repeatedly built strong technical teams and developed efficient organizational structures that maximize revenues. He is a technical editor for the
http://www.ember.com/products/techguide/standards.html IEEE 802.15.4 standards specification. He received a patent for self-organizing networks in 2000 and was awarded his Ph.D. from MIT in 2001.
