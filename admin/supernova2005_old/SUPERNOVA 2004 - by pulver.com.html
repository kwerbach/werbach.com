Supernova 2004 will be held June 24-25 in Santa Clara, CA.

Supernova explores the decentralization of communications, software, and media. It brings together approximately 200 leading-edge executives, technologists, investors, and other thought leaders in a unique, interactive format.

There is no better place to learn about the next generation of technology-driven change. The most dynamic, most innovative leaders of the high-tech world come to Supernova to network and to understand the emerging trends that will reshape their industries.

Why decentralization?
Intelligence is moving to the edges, through networked computers, empowered users, shifting partnerships, fluid digital content, distributed work teams, and powerful communications devices. From WiFi to Weblogs, connected home media to Web services, the landscape is changing. Business models for content, telecom, and information technology are under pressure as end-users gain greater control. Conflicts are heating up over intellectual property, voice over IP, security, and wireless spectrum allocation, among other topics.

Supernova is the only event that puts these issues in context. We assemble the most compelling people and companies from the converging worlds of computing, telecom, and digital media.

A uniquely valuable experience
At Supernova, we use the very technologies we talk about. We pioneered group Weblogs and wireless Internet links as integral elements of the physical conference. We'll continue to push the envelope with new tools at Supernova 2004. This is not one of those events where speakers drone on through canned presentations. Every attendee is an active participant.
