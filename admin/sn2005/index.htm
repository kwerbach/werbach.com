Welcome to Supernova 2005!
June 20-22, San Francisco, CA

Supernova has always been about making connections in an increasingly distributed world. Industry leaders agree, it is the place where...

• Top thought leaders and innovators reveal emerging trends in decentralized computing, media and communications

• Over 250 prominent technologists, investors, business leaders and media come to network

• Supernova community members across the world interact online using cutting-edge social software

Sign up for the Supernova Report or our conference update email list to hear more about the event and its themes.

What's new in 2005: A lot!

Business leadership
We're pleased to announce that Supernova is now produced in partnership with The Wharton School, one of the world's leading business schools. This year's conference will bring together visionaries, strategists and implementors for a unique perspective on decentralization trends.

New programs
We've added the Wharton West Workshops and Technology Showcase - a day of emerging technology workshops targeted to technologists and business professionals, along with an opening networking event, all held at Wharton's state-of-the-art facility in San Francisco, CA. Go to Wharton West's Web site for a tour. We'll also unveil new interactive tools to enhance the Supernova experience.


Connecting worlds through connected platforms

Our 2005 theme hones in on the fact that, in a decentralized world, the network itself is the platform for commerce, social interaction, work and entertainment. We'll explore the next generation of connected technologies in social and business contexts.

Fresh perspectives
We'll examine how corporations and individuals leverage social software, mobile devices, and online gaming environments, as well as ways that communities can maximize the latent value of their network connections. Topics include:

distributed e-commerce • mobile applications • the new power of the "long tail" in commerce and media • massively multiplayer virtual worlds • business blogging • pervasive wireless sensors • rich media collaboration • voice over IP applications • and more

Compelling participants
