Supernova is more than just a conference. The physical gathering in Santa Clara is only one element of an interactive, networked community. Using cutting-edge online community tools, we facilitate a parallel virtual event that extends Supernova in time, space, and participation.

Most of the tools below are accessible to non-attendees. Though there is no substitute for the on-site experience, we value participation from those who could not attend this year.

Conference Weblog
The Supernova blog provides conference coverage as well as commentary by a distinguished group of expert bloggers. Before the event, it will serve as a discussion forum and information resource.

Group Metablog
Point to postings on your own weblog. Use TrackBack URL <http://snblog.pulver.com/cgi-bin/mt-tb.cgi/37>, or the Web form on the metablog page.

Moblog
Post your digital photos to a shared album. Send photos directly from your cameraphone or as an email attachment to <supernova2004@blogs.com>.

Web Conferencing
Use Convoq ASAP - presence-enabled web conferencing - to meet anytime, anywhere with anyone in the Supernova community. Supernova attendees will receive instructions on how to access Convoq ASAP upon registering for the conference. Attendees will be pre-populated in your ASAP contact list, so you can collaborate and conduct meetings whenever you like. Add your personal contacts to your ASAP list and meet with them too! Trusted Connections
A special Supernova group for business networking within LinkedIn, the leading professional network tool online. Leverage the Supernova network to find and reach business contacts.
