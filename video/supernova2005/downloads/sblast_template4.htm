Connecting Worlds Through Connected Platforms

Technological and business trends are converging to change the way we work, live, and play. Did you know that:

  • There are more than 10 million active weblogs, a number doubling every five months? 
  • The largest type of traffic on the global Internet is video peer-to-peer file sharing?
  • You may soon get your TV from a phone company, your phone service from a software company, and your Internet access from your city government?
  • More cameras were sold last year in mobile phones than as stand-alone film or digital cameras?
  • The annual market for buying and selling "virtual assets" in multi-player online games is over $800 million per year?

Supernova 2005 addresses the causes and implications of these startling developments. Our sessions and workshops will cover important topics poised to jump from the leading edge to the mainstream, including:

  • Lightweight, collaborative online tools for "connected work" in an increasingly fast-moving, decentralized, globalized business environment.
  • New technologies and business models for pervasive broadband connectivity.
  • How the emerging video Internet and the explosion of blogging and grassroots journalism will turn the media industry upside down.
  • Potential killer applications for a world of pervasive mobile connectivity, such as podcasting, moblogging, voice over IP, online photo sharing, and mobile games.
  • Making sense of the cacophony of information and conversations flowing across networks.
