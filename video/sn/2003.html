Supernova 2003 was held July 8-9, 2003 in the Washington, DC area.

Supernova 2003 was a tremendous success, bringing together innovators, policy-makers and businesspeople for thought-provoking discussions and valuable networking. 

Our outstanding speaker list included:

- Reed Hundt, former FCC Chairman
- Joichi Ito, CEO, Neoteny Co. Ltd. (Japan)
- Kevin Lynch, Chief Software Architect, Macromedia
- Vice Admiral Dennis McGinn, US Navy (retired)
- J.C. Herz, Joystick Nation
- Merrill Brown, SVP, RealNetworks
- David Isenberg, Isen.com
- Ellen Levy, Neocarta Ventures
- Craig Silverstein, Director of Technology, Google
- Marko Ahtisaari, Insight & Foresight Unit, Nokia
- Bruce Mehlman, Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Tech Policy
- Mena Trott, CEO, Six Apart
- Simon Phipps, Chief Technology Evangelist, Sun Microsystems
- Sriram Viswanathan, Managing Director, Intel Capital
- John Blair, Chairman, Kenamea
- Mike Hirshland, General Partner, Polaris Venture Partners
- Nikolaj Nyholm, Founder & CTO, Ascio Technologies (Denmark)
- Gigi Sohn, President, Public Knowledge
- Bob Pepper, Chief of Policy Development, FCC
- Clay Shirky, Author and Consultant  

Supernova 2003 covered a wide range of cutting-edge topics, including:
  • Making (Business) Sense of Networks
  • No More Rear-View Mirrors: Investing in a Changed Environment
  • The Wireless Frontier
  • New Platforms, New User Experiences
  • Decentralized Identity and Security
  • Enterprise IT: Throwing Rocks in the Glass House
  • The Next Communications Industry
  • Tech Policy Outlook

Some of the best sessions weren't even on the official agenda, including a surprise visit from Google co-founder Sergey Brin, and an improptu roundtable of key Federal Communications Commission staff.
