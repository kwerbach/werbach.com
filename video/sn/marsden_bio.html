Christopher Marsden
Acting Internet Projects Officer, Oxford Internet Institute

Christopher Marsden is a prolific cyberlaw writer, researcher and consultant, who joined the Oxford Internet Institute on 1 May 2004. His research at OII focuses on cyberlaw and international political economy/Internet governance, broadband mobile Internet regulation and policy.

His latest publications are at http://www.selfregulation.info as well as chapters in 'Internet Television' (ed. Eli Noam et al, 2003) and 'Digital TV in Europe' (eds Picard and Brown, 2004, with Monica Arino) both published by Lawrence Erlbaum. He has edited the following collections of essays: "Convergence in European Digital TV Regulation" (Blackstone, London June 1999, with Stefaan Verhulst) and "Regulating the Global Information Society" (Routledge, 2000).

He is an Editorial Board Member of the journal 'info' (2003-date) and the Phoenix Center for Advanced Law and Public Policy Studies (2001-date). Previously he was a fellow at Warwick Business School (2001-2), the Globalisation Centre at Warwick (2000-1), the Harvard Information Infrastructure Project (1999-2000) and was a founding co-editor of International Journal of Communications Law and Policy (1998-2001) www.ijclp.org He was a consultant for the Council of Europe in 1999, having chaired the ESRC European Communications Research Group 1997-9. He has held teaching posts at London and Warwick Universities (1994-2000), and visiting research roles at universities on three continents. He has private sector managerial experience with Media Week Ltd (1989-91), Euromoney (1993-4), Shortmedia (2000-1) and WorldCom UK (2001-2).

He was the project manager of the IAPCODE project in 2004 and a Research Fellow at the Programme in Comparative Media Law & Policy. A frequent speaker at international conferences, he will be presenting the IAPCODE final results at several conferences in Europe and North America in the spring-summer of 2004.
