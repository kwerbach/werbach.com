Shawn Hardin
Senior Vice President, AOL Broadband

Shawn Hardin oversees development of AOL For Broadband, the world's most popular online service designed specifically for high-speed cable or DSL connections. In this role he leads the definition and business requirements for broadband-specific product, applications, content and programming. Mr. Hardin reports to Kevin Conroy, Chief Operating Officer, AOL Broadband.

Prior to joining AOL, Mr. Hardin spent seven years working with the National Broadcasting Company (NBC) and is the former EVP, Chief Product Officer and Group General Manager for NBC Internet. At NBCi, Hardin oversaw all Product Development, Design & Usability, Research, and Interactive Content. During his tenure, online usage grew to over seventeen million unique monthly visitors who spent 275 million minutes per month with NBCi products and services. Prior to NBCi, Mr. Hardin was the Senior Vice President of Snap.com -- a venture jointly owned by NNBC and CNET - where he managed the Engineering, Product, Programming and Design Departments. In this role he also led the development of convergence and broadband opportunities with television, cable, and telecommunication platforms.

Before Snap.com, Mr. Hardin worked at NBC (General Electric) where he co-founded two entrepreneurial divisions. As Vice President and Executive Producer of NBC Digital Productions, he oversaw both online content and enhanced-television services. Prior to NBC Digital Productions, Mr. Hardin was the Supervising Producer & Creative Director of NBC-2000, an on-air marketing and promotions group, managing all primetime short-form programming, seamless programming flow, and interstitial network branding.

Before joining NBC, Mr. Hardin worked as a freelance cinematographer, editor, producer and director on more than 70 film & video productions with companies such as MTV, Columbia Tristar, 20th Century Fox, and Turner Pictures, among others. His interactive and traditional media work has received multiple awards and honors, including recognition from the American Film Institute, Leeds, Bombay, CASIE, CableACE, ID Magazine, and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences.

As a Board member for the Academy of Television Arts & Sciences, Mr. Hardin oversaw creation of the industry's first Emmy Award recognizing creative excellence in Interactive Programming. He is also a Director member of the Directors Guild of America and sits on the Advisory Boards for the American Film Institute's Enhanced Television Program and the Producers Guild of America's New Media Council.

Mr. Hardin is a graduate of the University of Southern California, School of Cinema/TV.
